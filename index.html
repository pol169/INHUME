III. FIELD TRANSCRIPT: LOGISTICS OF THE DAMNED

Recovered Archive: April 30, 2026 // Lead Systems Architect
Status: RECONSTRUCTED FROM FRAGMENTED DATA TAPES

CHAPTER 1: THE KOLYMA-PACIFIC CONTRACT

The Kolyma-Pacific Archipelago was never meant to be a permanent home. To the outside world, it was an ambitious deep-sea oil and mining venture. To the administrative mainframe of The Company, it was designated as Classified Zone 4: a 32x32km laboratory for the most aggressive experiments in corporate history.

The labor force for Zone 4 was not recruited from prestigious universities or elite military units. Instead, The Company targeted the ”Deprived”. These were individuals from separate fields whose specialized skills were as valuable as they were expendable.

  • The Night-Club Guard: Hired for their passive ability, ”Unflinching Presence,” allowing them to body-block threats without knockback. (The Shield of Unit-5)
  • The Plumber: A ”Pocket Engineer” capable of automatically highlighting valves, wires, and structural weaknesses in the industrial ruins.
  • The Deep Diver: Trained to work in complete isolation. They have "made friends in the dark," granting them superior visibility in the toxic fog.
  • The Fast Food Employee: Possesses the "Happy Meal" passive for sanity recovery. Surprisingly agile on linoleum surfaces (10% speed boost).

CHAPTER 1.4: THE EXTRACTION MANDATE

Workers were told their primary mission was the recovery of copper wire and classified scavenged logs. They were informed that ”The cargo fights back,” and that the extraction was the boss fight.

This narrative was a mask. The Company didn’t send Unit 5 to save the world; they sent them because their replacement was already on the helicopter. You were never the hero; you were a budget line item.

CHAPTER 2: LIFE IN THE ATOLLS

Before the Tuesday Incident, the Kolyma-Pacific Archipelago was a monument to the mundane. The Company utilized ”Voxel Stamping” to nest hand-crafted residential and commercial prefabs into the procedural hillsides. To the personnel, these islands were not a military zone, but a series of ”Company Towns” designed to simulate a life of routine in an otherwise hostile environment.

The Architecture of Mundanity: Life in the Atolls was an exercise in the ”Art of the Fake.” The buildings—barracks, fast-food joints, and supply hubs—were constructed as modular prefabs. This stylized architecture allowed the Company to maintain a ”goofy” silhouette for character recognition while bathing the environment in gritty, atmospheric lighting to mask the underlying decay.

CHAPTER 3: THE CLASSIFIED MANDATE

While the Atolls provided a hollow simulation of comfort, the islands’ foundations were rotting with industrial secrets. Beyond the ”Logistics of the Damned” visible to the low-level workers, the Company executed a classified mandate that viewed the laws of physics and ethics as mere variables to be exploited for a higher bottom line.

The Subterranean Breach: The deep-sea drilling project was never intended for oil extraction; it was a focused search for a ”Pocket of Non-Space” located deep within the voxel crust. Experimental thermal-cutting tools were used to pierce the bedrock, regardless of the geological instability reported by the Plumber teams.

CHAPTER 4: THE 03:00 ALARM

The transition from a ”Cozy Horror” tension to a literal nightmare occurred at precisely 03:00 on a Tuesday. The ”Tuesday Incident” was not a single explosion, but a localized rupture of physics that occurred when the deep-crust drill at the center of the archipelago pierced a pocket of non-space.

To the workers on the graveyard shift, the first sign was not sound, but the sky—the horizon turned a permanent, sickly emerald glow, and the radioactive green fog became so thick it physically muffled the sound of the emergency sirens.

CHAPTER 5: THE SANITIZATION PROTOCOL

The Company’s response to the Tuesday Incident was governed by a single guiding principle: ”You are not the hero. You are a budget line item.” While the personnel in the Atolls looked to the sea for rescue, the Executives were already executing the Sanitization Protocol.

The Executive Flight: The evacuation was never intended for the labor force. As reality destabilized, the wealthy managers and lead scientists boarded private helicopters and sealed the facilities behind them. The heavy blast doors of the bunkers were locked from the outside, trapping the workforce in sectors that were rapidly being overtaken by mutated flora and radioactive fauna.

CHAPTER 6: THE GHOST IN THE MACHINE

As the official Company communications went dark, the surviving personnel utilized their technical skills to hack the 1980s CRT terminals scattered across the ruins. They transformed the archipelago’s infrastructure of deception into a desperate survivor network known as the **BBS Network**.

The Credit Economy: Survivors utilized magnetic corporate ID badges to track a localized digital currency, trading ”Credits” for essential survival items like D-Cell batteries and fuel. Terminals are now used to post contracts between survivors, ranging from plumbing repairs in toxic sectors to the dangerous retrieval of classified data tapes.

CHAPTER 10: THE ASH PERIMETER (TIER 1)

The outermost ring represents the ”Great Deception.” It is the most deceptively ”normal” sector, serving as the buffer between the infinite water blockade and the industrial rot of the interior.

The islands are characterized by low-sloping hills and monochrome beaches. The ground is composed of a soft, shifting carpet of gray soot, often several meters deep. This ”soot-soil” acts as a natural acoustic dampener; footsteps are swallowed entirely, creating an eerie, unnatural silence.

The weather is defined by the ”Dry Rain”—a constant, heavy fallout of radioactive ash. The sky is a perpetual twilight of bruised purple and charcoal gray. Visibility is moderately high, but the air is biologically abrasive.

CHAPTER 11: THE SINKING SILOS (TIER 2)

As the player pushes inward, the soft soot gives way to the brutalist concrete and rusted iron of Tier 2. This is the industrial engine of the Company, now collapsing under the weight of its own failures.

During the Tuesday Incident, the spacetime rupture at Ground Zero caused a ”Density Sink,” where the bedrock physically liquefied. Multi-ton concrete silos are actively, albeit slowly, sinking into the voxel seabed.

The atmosphere is a thick, dark soup of oil mist and radioactive vapor. The water reflects a sickly emerald glow from the sky. The humidity is highly corrosive; untreated steel boat hulls will show visible pitting within hours.

CHAPTER 12: THE CRYO-WASTES (TIER 3)

Tier 3 represents a sudden and violent shift. The transition from the emerald heat of the Silos to the sub-zero permafrost of the Wastes is instantaneous, marked by the ”Coolant Line Wall.”

The islands here are jagged pillars of frozen voxel-mud, ice, and scrap metal. The ground is slick with ”Black Ice”—a mixture of frozen oil and seawater. The terrain is filled with deep ice-cracks and ”Cold-Welded” ruins where industrial equipment has been fused into the ice in mid-operation.

The weather is a relentless, high-pressure blizzard. The howling winds create ”Audio Isolation,” making it impossible to hear anything more than a meter away—a state weaponized by the Whisperspine.

CHAPTER 13: GROUND ZERO (THE HEART)

The center of the archipelago is no longer part of our reality. It is the site of the original 1988 breach, where the Company’s greed finally pierced the veil of non-space.

The islands are a pulsating, deep-red nightmare of bio-organic fusion. The sand has been vitrified into ”Voxel Glass”—jagged, translucent shards that glow with inner rhythmic light. Gravity here is merely a suggestion; water flows in reverse spirals, and boulders drift lazily through the air.

The sky is a permanent, swirling tear in reality. Spacetime hazards are constant; survivors may experience ”Time-Slips” or ”Position-Swaps” where they are suddenly teleported several meters in a random direction.

CHAPTER 15: THE ESCAPE THEORY

Within the BBS network, the ”Secret Brokers” trade in the ”Escape Theory”—the speculative science of how to breach the Company’s infinite blockade. They maintain that the naval blockade is a ”Signature-Based” system. The only way to leave is to physically alter your boat’s ”Signature” at the Central Mainframe.

The Drowning Paradox: The Brokers hypothesize that the outer radius is a spacetime ”loop.” When a vessel reaches the 16km mark without the proper ”Clock Out” code, the anomaly simply resets the vessel’s position to 100 meters below the surface. You don’t sink; you are simply repositioned into the abyss.

CHAPTER 16: THE VOXEL ANOMALY & GROUND ZERO

The "Tuesday Incident" (03:00) was the result of a catastrophic failure during a deep-crust extraction. When the drill pierced the non-space pocket, the resulting nuclear flare fused the islands' bedrock into **Voxel Glass**—a transparent, high-radiation substance that now forms the skeleton of the archipelago.

The Mainframe Protocol: To bypass the Company's automated naval blockade, survivors must reach the central Mainframe at Ground Zero. Only by "Clocking Out" at this terminal can a vessel's signature be authorized for exit through the anomaly loop.

The Drowning Paradox (Expanded): Vessels that attempt to breach the perimeter without authorization are caught in a spacetime reset. You do not die; you are simply repositioned 100 meters below the surface, trapped in a recursive cycle of drowning until the anomaly decides to spit you back out.
